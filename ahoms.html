600 years of
Ahom Kingdom
by Supriya Dutta
The six hundred years of Ahom kingdom from 1228 to 1822 is a glorious chapter in Assam History. Sukupha along with the team of nine hundred soldiers, fifteen elite members left from Maolung, China and entered Assam in 1228. Assam, located in 24째07' to 28째00 N and 89째42'E to 96째02'E is state with its territory covering mostly a fertile plain area with the mighty river Brahmaputra and its tributary. Sukapha established a small kingdom covering some reas of the present Sivasagar and Dibrugarh district of Upper Assam. The different communities in the state during that time were the Moran and the Borahi under their kings. Another two powerful kingdom nearby were the Chutia in the north eastern corner and the Kachari in the western side.

The kings were elected through two procedures: Primogeniture and through ministers. Patra mantris and Pali manstris had the power to depose and elect kings in the Ahom Kingdom

In this later part of the timeperiod, we can see that, most kings were elected through ministers and deposed as well

Two of those Kings even had on 1 month and 20 days respectively as their duration of Reign
